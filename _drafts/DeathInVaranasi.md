Varanasi is known as a sacred Hindu place, and as a result burning

As I neared the pyre, a young man approached me trying to act as my guide. I told him it was not needed, but I didn't totally wave him off. I struggle with this, the balance of being kind and also not being seen as easy prey for a scam. Both because I don't want to be overly bothered, and because I don't want to appear to be a promising lead. 

I was curious to observe the proceeding more closely and didn't know the proper etiquette. Another person who made themselves look official greeted me and told me they would show me a proper place to observe without interupting. In this case, as an outsider I obliged. He explained to me the particular wood they used, how there were many people nearby on death's door, waiting to be honored by this proceeding.

The only dead bodies I had seen were family members who had just passed. I had never seen so many and treated both respectfully and casually. The caste system was in full effect: some bodies were ornately wrapped as the went on the pyre, others were just covered in cloth. They all burned just the same.

I was curious into various Buddhist practices, and a core one is contemplation of death. *charnal grounds*

So I sat there quietly, meditating on a particular body. Smoke would come toward me, and it was hard not to think about the fact it was the smoke from a body burning. I'd close my eyes and let the smoke pass, and continue sitting and reflecting. While it was meant as a honorary thing, there were the logistics of moving all the bodies, different people and families involved with each one. It was interesting to observe my reaction to it all, but I also didn't want to overstay my welcome so left after the particular body was no longer recognizable.

What came next was not what I expected, I had expected to give the guide something for his time, but hadn't realized the extent of it. He took me to his "boss" and explained how in order to support the wood for the lower caste people, they needed a lot of money. Luckily I wasn't carrying much on me. In the moment it felt a little off, but I wasn't sure enough to totally dismiss them as I had the younger guide earlier. I gave them the equivalent of about $20, which partly felt justified for the guy showing me around. They were adamant that this was insufficient and wanted like 10 times the amount. At this point it did feel off and I left.

As I was leaving and pretty sure it was a scam, I took a different route wanting to avoid my first "guide" who had really wanted me to check out silk shop.

I took what I thought was a slightly roundabout way, but he must have been sort of tracking me because he found me as I got back to the general area of the Ghat. Guilt vs reality.



The Paramis of Patience

This was a common form of interaction in Varanasi that was frustrating, what ended up being a lose-lose situation. The money was not significant, but rather the fact that both parties left annoyed. They wanted more out of me and I just wanted to be left alone.
